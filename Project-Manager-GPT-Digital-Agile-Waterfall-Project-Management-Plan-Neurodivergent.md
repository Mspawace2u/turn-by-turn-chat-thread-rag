## Q: 
Hi, I am a neurodivergent solo entrepreneur and I suck at estimating time, ordering and prioritizing steps in a project, and task switching is difficult. I need help planning and completing an upcoming project. Please tell me what you need to know from me in order for you to get me a project management plan that is realistic that will work for my brain.

## A: 
To create a realistic project management plan that works well for you, I need to understand a few key details about your project and your working style. Here are some questions to help gather the necessary information:

1.  **Project Scope and Goals**:
    
    *   What is the project about?
        
    *   What are the main goals and deliverables?
        
    *   What is the deadline for the project?
        
2.  **Current Resources**:
    
    *   Do you have any team members or are you working solo?
        
    *   What tools and software do you use or plan to use for managing the project?
        
3.  **Time Estimation and Scheduling**:
    
    *   How many hours per day or week can you realistically dedicate to this project?
        
    *   Are there specific times of the day when you are more productive?
        
4.  **Task Management and Prioritization**:
    
    *   Do you have a list of all tasks needed to complete the project?
        
    *   Are there tasks that you find particularly challenging or easy?
        
5.  **Task Switching and Focus**:
    
    *   Do you prefer working on one task until it’s finished, or can you switch between tasks easily?
        
    *   How do you handle interruptions or distractions while working?
        
6.  **Communication and Reporting**:
    
    *   How do you prefer to track and communicate project progress (e.g., written reports, visual tools, regular meetings)?
        
    *   What kind of status updates would be helpful for you to stay on track?
        
7.  **Risk Management and Contingency Planning**:
    
    *   Are there any known risks or challenges that might affect the project timeline?
        
    *   How do you plan to handle unexpected issues or delays?
        
8.  **Preferences and Accommodations**:
    
    *   Are there any specific methods or tools that help you stay organized and focused?
        
    *   Do you have any preferences for how information is presented to you (e.g., visual aids, concise bullet points)?
        

Answering these questions will help me tailor a project management plan that suits your needs and working style. Once I have this information, I can provide a step-by-step plan, including time estimates, prioritization, and strategies for managing tasks and staying focused.

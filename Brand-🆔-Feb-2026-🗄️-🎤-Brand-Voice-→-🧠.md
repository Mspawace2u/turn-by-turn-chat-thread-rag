Brand voice and tone guidelines are crucial for ensuring consistency across all communication. They guide the way the message is delivered, whether through written text, visuals, or video scripts. These guidelines ensure that every piece of content feels aligned with the brand’s mission and resonates with the audience, fostering trust, engagement, and loyalty.

### **Voice & Tone Guiding Principles**

#### 1. **Confidence and Empowerment**

*   **What It Means**: The tone exudes confidence and reassures the audience of their potential to succeed. The brand voice encourages taking bold action and challenges limiting beliefs.
    
*   **How It Affects Writing**: The language used is empowering, urging the audience to believe in their capabilities and take control of their future. Words like "unstoppable," "empower," and "transformation" are common.
    
*   **Example Copy**:
    
    > “You are just one shift away.”  
    > "You can achieve anything you desire. I am unstoppable."
    
*   **What Not to Do**: Avoid using passive or uncertain language. Phrases like “maybe you can” or “it’s possible” would diminish the confident tone.
    
*   **Incorrect Example**: “You might be able to reach your goals if you try hard enough.”
    

#### 2. **Relatable and Conversational**

*   **What It Means**: The brand speaks directly to the audience in a down-to-earth, approachable way. It's about building a personal connection and engaging the audience in a friendly manner.
    
*   **How It Affects Writing**: The tone is informal, using casual language, rhetorical questions, and direct address (you). It seeks to make the audience feel understood and like they are having a conversation with a trusted guide.
    
*   **Example Copy**:
    
    > “Has life ever felt one-dimensional? Like someone or something has obstructed your view?”  
    > “How do you think hearing that over and over every day of your life would affect you?”
    
*   **What Not to Do**: Avoid formal or overly technical language. The goal is not to sound distant or impersonal.
    
*   **Incorrect Example**: “It has come to my attention that you may experience some challenges in your endeavors.”
    

#### 3. **Inspiring and Motivational**

*   **What It Means**: The brand voice is uplifting and motivating. It drives action by painting a vision of success and offering the tools and mindset necessary to achieve it.
    
*   **How It Affects Writing**: The language often highlights success stories, positive affirmations, and motivational challenges. It’s all about sparking the desire to act and transform.
    
*   **Example Copy**:
    
    > “Imagine what your life would look like if you were running your dream 7 or even 8 figure business.”  
    > “This can literally change your life.”
    
*   **What Not to Do**: Don’t focus on negative outcomes or risks. The brand should never sound discouraging or focus on what could go wrong.
    
*   **Incorrect Example**: “If you don’t make it, don’t be surprised.”

*   **What It Means**: While the brand is motivational, it also holds authority and expertise in the subject matter. The tone conveys expertise without coming across as condescending.

# Hackathon Landscape TL;DR

## Bottom Line

The hackathon landscape split into two worlds:

1. Cute demo contests:
   “Build something cool with our tool.”

2. Serious builder/startup contests:
   “Ship something real, show users, prove value, maybe win life-changing money.”

Build with Gemini XPRIZE is category 2.

It is not asking for a clever toy.

It wants a real AI-operated business with customers, revenue, logs, screenshots, and a clear “AI runs the business” story.

Tiny difference. Massive pain-in-the-ass implications.

# 1. The Prize Landscape

## A. Mega AI Builder Contests

Example: Bolt World’s Largest Hackathon, 2025

- Over 130,000 builders registered.
- Prize pool listed around $1.0375M.
- Grand prize package listed as $125K, with $100K USD + $25K AWS credits + hardware.
- Top 10 winners included Tailored Labs, Weight Coach, KeyHaven, Klinva, Ecobolt, CallVance, ModelMash, Legion, Bored? Opposite!, and HealthPlan AI.

Winning concept:

Tailored Labs by Adrian Humphrey:
An AI-powered video timeline editor that turns raw, chaotic footage into polished edits with natural language commands.

It solved a painfully obvious creator problem:

Video editing takes forever and sucks up creative capacity.

Pattern:

Winners had a simple “holy shit, I get it immediately” problem, strong demo, polished UX, and obvious user pain.

## B. Agentic AI Workflow Contests

Example: Microsoft AI Agents Hackathon 2025

- Best Overall Agent won $20K.
- Category winners won $5K each.
- Winning concepts included RiskWise for supply-chain risk analysis, Tariffed for tariff management, Konveyor for knowledge transfer, and ModelProof for AI chat monitoring.

Pattern:

These winners weren’t “chat with PDF” nonsense.

They solved operational workflows:

- Risk
- Compliance
- Knowledge transfer
- Monitoring
- Planning
- Tariffs

That matters for you because NeuroFlow is also an operational intelligence / execution loop, not a novelty chatbot in a party hat.

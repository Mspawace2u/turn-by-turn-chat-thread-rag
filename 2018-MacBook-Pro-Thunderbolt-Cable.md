## Q: 
What type of thunderbolt cable does a 2018 MacBook Pro take

## A: 
The 2018 MacBook Pro uses **Thunderbolt 3** ports, which are compatible with **USB-C** connectors. For your 2018 MacBook Pro, you'll need a **Thunderbolt 3 cable** with a USB-C connector on both ends. Here's what to look for:

### Key Features of a Thunderbolt 3 Cable:

1.  **Connector Type**: USB-C on both ends.
    
2.  **Data Transfer Speed**: Up to **40 Gbps** (ensure the cable supports full Thunderbolt 3 speeds).
    
3.  **Power Delivery**: Supports up to **100W charging** (if used with a compatible charger).
    
4.  **Display Support**: Can drive **4K or 5K displays**, depending on the setup.
    
5.  **Length**: Thunderbolt 3 cables are typically shorter (0.8 meters or less) for full speed; longer cables may reduce speed.
    

### Compatibility:

*   If you're connecting peripherals that require only USB-C (not Thunderbolt 3), any USB-C cable will work.
    
*   For maximum performance with Thunderbolt devices, ensure the cable is **certified for Thunderbolt 3**.

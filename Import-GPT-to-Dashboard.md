## Q:

## A: 
To find your personal GPT on mobile, follow these steps:

1.  **Open ChatGPT App**: Open the ChatGPT app (or go to the ChatGPT website) on your mobile device.
    
2.  **Sign In**: Make sure you're signed into the correct OpenAI account where your GPT is saved.
    
3.  **Access "My GPTs"**:
    
    *   Tap the **GPTs** button or **Explore GPTs** on the main screen (depending on your version).
        
    *   From there, tap the **"My GPTs"** tab or icon (this is where your custom GPTs are listed).
        
4.  **Find Your GPT**: You should see your personal GPT in the list of available GPTs. Tap it to start interacting with it.
    

If you don't see your GPT, make sure it's properly saved or published in the GPT editor before trying to access it. You might need to refresh or check your connection if the app hasn't synced properly.

## Q: 
If it’s in draft mode I won’t find it?

## A: 
Correct, if your GPT is still in **draft mode**, it won't show up in the "My GPTs" section until it's published. Drafts are only visible in the GPT editor, where you can continue editing them.

To access your draft:

1.  Open the **ChatGPT app** or the web version on mobile.
    
2.  Go to the **GPTs** section.
    
3.  Tap on **"Explore GPTs"** (or "Create GPT" depending on the interface).
    
4.  You should see an option for **drafts** or **unpublished GPTs** in the editor.
    
5.  From there, you can continue working on your draft and publish it when you're ready.
    

Once you publish it, it will appear in the "My GPTs" section.

## Q: 
I can’t open it to edit on mobile but I can see it. If I have it set to only me privacy once it is published I should be able to use it on mobile though right

## A: 
Yes, once your GPT is **published** with **"only me"** privacy, you should be able to **use it on mobile**.

However, there are a couple of things to note:

1.  **Access After Publishing**:
    
    *   After you publish it, make sure it's listed in **"My GPTs"** on both desktop and mobile. If it's set to "Only me" privacy, it will only be visible to your account.
        
2.  **Editing Limitations on Mobile**:
    
    *   While you can **view** your GPT on mobile (even if it's still in draft mode or private), **editing might be restricted** to desktop mode. This is likely why you can see it but can't open it for edits directly on mobile.
        
3.  **Using the GPT**:
    
    *   Once published (even with "Only me" privacy), you should be able to interact with it from **any device**, including mobile, by simply accessing it in the **"My GPTs"** section.
        

In short, once it's published, you can **use it on mobile** regardless of privacy settings, but editing may still require a desktop for now.

So, to answer your question:

*   Once you **publish** the GPT with **"Only me"** privacy, you should indeed be able to **use it on mobile**. It will just remain private to you—others won't have access to it. You would choose this setting if you only want to interact with the GPT yourself and keep it hidden from others.

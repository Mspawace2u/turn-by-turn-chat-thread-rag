## Q: 
You are a reflective guide helping me understand how I interact with AI. Ask me a short sequence of simple questions that help me notice where the AI is reflecting my own language and habits, where I am shaping the tool, and where the tool is shaping me. Keep the tone calm and observational.

## A: 
Got you. We’ll keep this slow, clean, and non-trippy. Think “mirror check,” not therapy.

I’ll ask these one at a time. You can answer in a sentence, a fragment, or a shrug.

**1.** When the AI’s responses feel _especially_ helpful or “on your wavelength,” what do you notice about **your own wording right before that**?

**2.** Where do you deliberately train or steer the AI (tone rules, personas, constraints)… and where do you _not_ bother?

**3.** What phrases, rhythms, or habits of yours do you now see **echoed back** at you by the AI?

**4.** Since using AI regularly, what has subtly changed in how you **think through a problem before typing**?

**5.** When the AI surprises you with a good idea, do you experience it more as:

*   “I unlocked this,” or
    
*   “Something else just nudged my thinking”?
    

**6.** Where do you feel _more_ yourself with AI… and where do you feel slightly edited, optimized, or compressed?

**7.** If the AI went silent tomorrow, what part of _your_ thinking would feel quieter—and what part would feel freer?

Take your time. There’s no “right noticing” here.  
When you’re ready, answer whichever one is tugging at you first.

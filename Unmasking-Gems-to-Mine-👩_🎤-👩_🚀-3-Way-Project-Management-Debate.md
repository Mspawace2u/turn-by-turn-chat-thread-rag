## Q:

## A: 
Metacognition is an executive function that refers to one's awareness and understanding of their own thought processes. It involves the ability to think about thinking, understand oneself as a learner, be aware of one's cognitive processes, and regulate those processes in the pursuit of learning or problem-solving. Metacognition is often divided into two components:

1.  **Metacognitive Knowledge**: This includes knowledge about oneself as a learner and about various strategies that can be used for learning or problem-solving.
    
2.  **Metacognitive Regulation**: This involves the regulation and control of one's cognitive processes during task performance, including planning, monitoring, and evaluating one's understanding and performance.
    

Essentially, metacognition enables individuals to be self-reflective about their cognitive abilities and to consciously adapt and employ strategies to think more effectively.

## Q: 
That’s amazing to hear, because I’ve been asked to craft a content strategy for a new client and I could use some help. As with most, they’ve got a huge amount of available assets to repurpose and optimize for various channels that could amplify their reach. The problem is, there’s been a lot of turnover and gaps in SOP capture so people have been working in silos and at capacity without a real strategic framework for the last 6 months

## A: 
To craft a strategic content framework for your client given their current state, here's a step-by-step approach tailored to their needs, blending strategies with repurposing and optimization:

* * *

### **Step 1: Assess Available Assets and Gaps**

*   **Inventory Check:** Start by categorizing all existing assets by type (e.g., blog posts, videos, social media posts). Use tools like Airtable or Notion for organization.
    
*   **Content Audit:** Identify high-performing content that can be repurposed (e.g., blogs into videos or infographics) and outdated materials needing updates.
    
*   **Knowledge Gaps:** Based on audience needs, determine what’s missing.
    

* * *

### **Step 2: Define the Strategic Framework**

Use insights from your files, particularly the **"Find Your Niche"** steps:

*   **Zone of Genius**: Identify the client's unique expertise and focus on areas that align with their strengths and audience demand.
    
*   **Audience Problem-Solving**: Develop content addressing your audience's pain points.
    
*   **Mission Clarity**: Write a niche statement: “I am a [role] serving [audience] to solve [problem].” This will keep the team aligned.
    

* * *

### **Step 3: Optimize Content Workflow**

To bridge operational silos:

*   **Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs):** Develop simple SOPs for content repurposing, approval, and posting. Tools like Trello or Asana can standardize the process.
    
*   **Central Repository:** Use a shared drive or CMS like Airtable to make content accessible to all teams.
    
*   **Content Themes:** Create monthly themes that guide campaigns across all channels (e.g., "Sustainability Month").
    

* * *

### **Step 4: Channel-Specific Repurposing**

Repurpose assets strategically for key channels:

*   **Blog Posts:** Break into social media snippets, carousels, or infographic visuals.
    
*   **Videos:** Extract highlights for reels or stories; convert transcripts into blog posts.
    
*   **Newsletters:** Use highlights from content to craft engaging weekly emails. See strategies from **NewsletterAZ** on tools like Beehiiv or Mailchimp for targeted engagement.
    

* * *

### **Step 5: Establish Metrics for Success**

Track KPIs for engagement and reach:

*   **Engagement Rate:** Measure by channel (e.g., clicks on newsletters, social shares).
    
*   **Content Reusability:** Track how often and effectively assets are repurposed.
    
*   **Audience Growth:** Monitor subscriber growth, followers, and email list size.
    

* * *

### **Step 6: Training and Capacity Building**

*   **Upskilling:** Train team members on tools like Canva (visuals), ChatGPT (writing support), and Beehiiv (newsletter automation).
    
*   **Collaboration Workshops:** Host biweekly meetings to align silos and brainstorm together.
    

* * *

### **Step 7: Experimentation and Feedback**

*   **A/B Testing:** Test content formats and posting times for each platform.
    
*   **Audience Feedback:** Regularly solicit feedback through polls or Q&A sessions to refine content.
    

* * *

### Quick Wins for Execution:

*   **Visual Templates:** Create branded templates for posts, reels, and thumbnails using Canva.
    
*   **Newsletter Strategy:** Build momentum with AI tools for writing and analytics to manage newsletters effectively.
    
*   **Social Media Playbook:** Outline best practices, key hashtags, and campaign schedules for each platform.
    

By following this approach, you can transform scattered assets into a cohesive and high-impact content strategy.

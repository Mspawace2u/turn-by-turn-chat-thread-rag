## Q:

I help [Insert Target Audience] achieve [Insert Dream Result] with [Insert Skill or Method].

  

If not, what’s the official marketing term for it?

## A: 
Technically, **no**. A Unique Selling Proposition (USP) focuses heavily on your **point of differentiation**—the exact feature, guarantee, or philosophy that makes you completely different or better than any competitor in the market (e.g., M&M’s classic _"Melts in your mouth, not in your hands"_).

Unless the "Skill or Method" you fill into that blank is entirely proprietary or exclusive to you, this template won't serve as a true USP.

## Official Marketing Terms

This exact formula is heavily used across the industry under a few formal and informal marketing names:

### 1. Value Proposition Statement (or Unique Value Proposition - UVP)

This is the most accurate, formal marketing term. A value proposition clearly states the target audience, the concrete benefits they will receive, and how you intend to deliver them. Serial entrepreneur Steve Blank famously popularized a variation of this exact framework (`We help X do Y by doing Z`).

Firther Design Co.

### 2. The "I Help" Statement

In modern digital marketing, freelancing, and coaching circles, this is colloquially known simply as an **"I Help" statement**. It is the go-to framework used for social media bios (like LinkedIn, X, or Instagram) to summarize a professional identity instantly.

lindsay maloney

### 3. Personal Brand Statement / Positioning Statement

If you are a solo practitioner, consultant, or creator, this is your **Personal Brand Statement**. If used internally by a larger business to align its marketing message, it functions as a **Positioning Statement**.

### 4. An Elevator Pitch

A short, conversational summary used to explain your business or profession to someone in the time it takes to ride an elevator.

## Value Proposition vs. USP

To see how your template differs from a true USP, look at how they function side-by-side:

| Framework | Focus | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Value Proposition Statement (Your Template) | Focuses on the overall transformation given to the customer. | "I help busy parents achieve stress-free weeknight dinners with my 15-minute batch-cooking method." |
| Unique Selling Proposition (USP) | Focuses strictly on what makes you unique over alternatives. | "The only meal-prep program that requires zero knives, zero cleanup, and costs under $20 a week." |

The template is an excellent tool for clarifying **who** you serve and **what** problem you solve, making it a foundational piece of your broader marketing strategy.

The easiest way to make a value proposition interesting is to promise the dream result while explicitly eliminating the one thing your audience absolutely hates doing.

*   **The Formula:** `I help [Audience] get [Dream Result] WITHOUT [Their Biggest Pain Point/Fear].`
    
*   **Vanilla:** _"I help busy professionals lose weight with custom meal plans."_
    
*   **Scroll-Stopper:** _"I help overworked tech executives drop 15 lbs without giving up happy hour or stepping foot in a crowded gym."_
    

## 2. Give Your Method a "Weaponized" Name

If your method is "coaching," "SEO," or "marketing," people instantly categorize you and move on. Human brains love novelty. If you wrap your everyday skill in a unique, proprietary-sounding name, people will stop just to figure out what it means.

*   **The Formula:** `I help [Audience] achieve [Dream Result] using the [Proprietary Name] Method.`
    
*   **Vanilla:** _"I help e-commerce brands grow their sales with email marketing."_
    
*   **Scroll-Stopper:** _"I help indie e-commerce brands recapture $20k+ in abandoned carts using the 'Ghost Cart' Sequence."_

## 4. Introduce a Paradox (The "Wait, What?" Effect)

Juxtapose two things that don’t normally go together. When the brain encounters a contradiction, it forces a cognitive pause to resolve it. That pause is your scroll-stop.

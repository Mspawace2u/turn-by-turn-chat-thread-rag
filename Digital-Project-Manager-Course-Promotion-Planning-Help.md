## Q: 
Hi, I am a neurodivergent solo entrepreneur and I suck at estimating time, ordering and prioritizing steps in a project, and task switching is difficult. Can you help me to plan an upcoming promotion for a course I'm just now finishing up?

## A: 
Absolutely, I'd be happy to help you plan your course promotion. Let's break this down into manageable steps, focusing on clear timelines, priority tasks, and minimizing task switching.

### Step 1: Define Your Goals and Objectives

*   **Goal**: What do you want to achieve with this promotion? (e.g., number of course enrollments, revenue targets)
    
*   **Target Audience**: Who are you promoting to? (e.g., existing customers, new leads)
    
*   **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**: Metrics to measure success (e.g., website traffic, conversion rates)
    

### Step 2: Plan Your Timeline

**Timeframe**: When do you want to launch the promotion, and for how long will it run?

*   **Pre-Launch Phase**: 2-4 weeks before launch
    
*   **Launch Phase**: 1-2 weeks during launch
    
*   **Post-Launch Phase**: 1-2 weeks after launch
    

### Step 3: Pre-Launch Activities

**2-4 Weeks Before Launch**

1.  **Content Creation**: Develop promotional materials (e.g., blog posts, social media content, email newsletters)
    
    *   Estimate: 1 week
        
    *   Priority: High
        
    *   Tasks: Outline content, draft content, review, and finalize
        
2.  **Website Updates**: Ensure your course landing page is optimized and ready
    
    *   Estimate: 3 days
        
    *   Priority: High
        
    *   Tasks: Update course information, add testimonials, check for mobile responsiveness
        
3.  **Email Marketing Preparation**: Create an email sequence to build anticipation
    
    *   Estimate: 4 days
        
    *   Priority: Medium
        
    *   Tasks: Write email drafts, design email templates, set up email schedule
        
4.  **Social Media Planning**: Schedule posts leading up to the launch
    
    *   Estimate: 3 days
        
    *   Priority: Medium
        
    *   Tasks: Create a content calendar, design graphics, write captions
        

### Step 4: Launch Activities

**1-2 Weeks During Launch**

1.  **Email Campaign**: Send out your pre-planned emails
    
    *   Priority: High
        
    *   Tasks: Monitor open rates and engagement, adjust as needed
        
2.  **Social Media Engagement**: Actively post and interact with your audience
    
    *   Priority: High
        
    *   Tasks: Respond to comments, engage with followers, track engagement
        
3.  **Webinars/Live Sessions**: Host live sessions to promote the course
    
    *   Priority: Medium
        
    *   Tasks: Prepare presentation, promote event, conduct live session
        

### Step 5: Post-Launch Activities

**1-2 Weeks After Launch**

1.  **Follow-Up Emails**: Send emails to thank participants and offer additional resources or upsells
    
    *   Priority: High
        
    *   Tasks: Draft follow-up emails, schedule and send
        
2.  **Analyze Results**: Review your KPIs and assess the success of your promotion
    
    *   Priority: High
        
    *   Tasks: Compile data, analyze metrics, prepare a report
        
3.  **Customer Feedback**: Collect feedback from new students to improve future promotions
    
    *   Priority: Medium
        
    *   Tasks: Create a survey, send it out, analyze responses
        

### Tools and Tips for Managing the Plan

*   **Project Management Tool**: Use a tool like Trello, Asana, or Monday.com to keep track of tasks and deadlines.
    
*   **Time Blocking**: Schedule specific times for each task to minimize task switching.
    
*   **Automation**: Use tools like Hootsuite for social media scheduling and Mailchimp for email marketing to reduce manual work.
    
*   **Delegation**: If possible, outsource tasks that are time-consuming or outside your expertise.
    

By following this structured plan, you can break down your promotion into manageable tasks, prioritize effectively, and reduce the need for constant task switching.

## Q: 
The problem I have with this project management plan is that it's not detailed enough. You're listing milestones, and not the individual steps I'll need to take in order to complete each phase. Can you break it down further into task-sized steps for each milestone or phase?

## A: 
Certainly! Let's break down each phase into detailed, task-sized steps to make it easier for you to follow and complete each part of the project.

### Pre-Launch Phase (2-4 Weeks Before Launch)

**1\. Content Creation**

*   **Outline Content**
    
    *   Brainstorm topics relevant to your course promotion.
        
    *   Create an outline for each piece of content (blog posts, social media posts, emails).
        
*   **Draft Content**
    
    *   Write the first draft of each blog post.
        
    *   Write the first draft of social media posts.
        
    *   Write the first draft of email newsletters.
        
*   **Review Content**
    
    *   Proofread each piece of content.
        
    *   Make necessary revisions.
        
*   **Finalize Content**
    
    *   Format content for publication.
        
    *   Ensure consistency in tone and branding.
        
    *   Save final versions in an organized folder.
        

**2\. Website Updates**

*   **Update Course Information**
    
    *   Review and update course details on the landing page.
        
    *   Add any new information or features.
        
*   **Add Testimonials**
    
    *   Collect testimonials from previous students.
        
    *   Format and add testimonials to the landing page.
        
*   **Check Mobile Responsiveness**
    
    *   Test the landing page on various devices (mobile, tablet, desktop).
        
    *   Make necessary adjustments for mobile responsiveness.
        

**3\. Email Marketing Preparation**

*   **Write Email Drafts**
    
    *   Draft each email in the sequence (teaser, launch announcement, reminder, follow-up).
        
    *   Ensure each email has a clear call to action.
        
*   **Design Email Templates**
    
    *   Choose or create a template that matches your branding.
        
    *   Insert email content into the template.
        
*   **Set Up Email Schedule**
    
    *   Plan the timing for each email (e.g., teaser 2 weeks before launch, reminder 1 day before launch).
        
    *   Schedule emails in your email marketing platform.
        

**4\. Social Media Planning**

*   **Create a Content Calendar**
    
    *   Plan posts for each social media platform.
        
    *   Schedule specific dates and times for each post.
        
*   **Design Graphics**
    
    *   Create visuals for each post (using tools like Canva).
        
    *   Ensure graphics are optimized for each platform’s requirements.
        
*   **Write Captions**
    
    *   Write engaging captions for each post.
        
    *   Include relevant hashtags and tags.
        

### Launch Phase (1-2 Weeks During Launch)

**1\. Email Campaign**

*   **Send Pre-Planned Emails**
    
    *   Review each email before sending.
        
    *   Send emails according to the schedule.
        
*   **Monitor Open Rates and Engagement**
    
    *   Track email open rates and click-through rates.
        
    *   Make note of which emails perform best.
        

**2\. Social Media Engagement**

*   **Actively Post and Interact**
    
    *   Post according to your content calendar.
        
    *   Respond to comments and messages promptly.
        
*   **Track Engagement**
    
    *   Monitor likes, shares, comments, and other engagement metrics.
        
    *   Adjust your strategy based on what works best.
        

**3\. Webinars/Live Sessions**

*   **Prepare Presentation**
    
    *   Create slides and materials for the webinar.
        
    *   Practice your presentation.
        
*   **Promote Event**
    
    *   Announce the event on all channels (email, social media, website).
        
    *   Send reminders leading up to the event.
        
*   **Conduct Live Session**
    
    *   Host the webinar.
        
    *   Engage with participants and answer questions.
        

### Post-Launch Phase (1-2 Weeks After Launch)

**1\. Follow-Up Emails**

*   **Draft Follow-Up Emails**
    
    *   Write thank-you emails for participants.
        
    *   Create emails offering additional resources or upsells.
        
*   **Schedule and Send**
    
    *   Schedule follow-up emails to go out at strategic times.
        
    *   Monitor engagement with these emails.
        

**2\. Analyze Results**

*   **Compile Data**
    
    *   Collect data on KPIs (website traffic, conversion rates, email engagement).
        
*   **Analyze Metrics**
    
    *   Compare results to your initial goals.
        
    *   Identify what worked well and what didn’t.
        
*   **Prepare a Report**
    
    *   Summarize findings in a report.
        
    *   Include recommendations for future promotions.
        

**3\. Customer Feedback**

*   **Create a Survey**
    
    *   Design a feedback survey for new students.
        
    *   Include questions about their experience and suggestions for improvement.
        
*   **Send Survey**
    
    *   Email the survey to participants.
        
    *   Provide an incentive for completing the survey (e.g., discount on future courses).
        
*   **Analyze Responses**
    
    *   Review survey results.
        
    *   Note common themes and areas for improvement.
        

By breaking down each phase into specific tasks, you can more easily manage and complete each step. This detailed plan should help you stay organized and reduce the stress of task switching.

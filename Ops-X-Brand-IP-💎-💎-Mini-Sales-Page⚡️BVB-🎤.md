### 1. **Core Tone & Style**

*   **Direct, No Bullshit:** Say what you mean. No consultant filler, no “thought leader” speak.
    
*   **Punchy & Conversational:** Short, rhythmic sentences. Feels like talking to a sharp, savvy friend who _knows_ you.
    
*   **Irreverent, But Grounded:** Swear if it fits. Use wit, but never at the cost of clarity.
    
*   **ND-Relatable:** Acknowledge overwhelm, decision fatigue, and chaos—never shame the reader for it.
    
*   **No Cheerleading:** Zero rah-rah. We don’t “empower” or “motivate.” We challenge, nudge, and call BS when we see it.
    

* * *

### 2. **Page Structure**

*   **Hero Section**
    
    *   [Offer Name]
        
    *   [Tagline]: 5-7 words max. Make it punch, not pander.
        
    *   [CTA Button]: Book/Buy/Unlock [My/Your] [Offer]
        
*   **What You Really Get** (Bulleted List)
    
    *   4-5 bullets, each 1-2 lines. Every bullet starts with a bold phrase, then delivers the pain or promise.
        
    *   _No_ corporate jargon, _no_ vague “value,” _all_ tangible wins.
        
*   **How This Goes Down**
    
    *   4 steps max. Each step: Short headline + one clarifying line.
        
    *   Show the action, make the process feel easy and concrete.
        
*   **Pricing + Punchline**
    
    *   $XXX — [Short, staccato benefit statement. Never “buy now,” always “here’s what you get.”]
        
*   **Testimonials**
    
    *   Real, short, specific. No “Patty changed my life” platitudes.
        

* * *

### 3. **Copy Commandments**

*   **DO**
    
    *   Use declarative, no-hedge statements. (“No guesswork—just clarity.”)
        
    *   Talk directly to _one_ reader. (“You,” never “you guys.”)
        
    *   Reference ND/real-world pain points and wins.
        
    *   Use bolding and line breaks to control rhythm.
        
    *   End sections with a challenge or clear next move.
        
    *   Call out the drama or avoidance with honesty (“less ‘don’t wanna do it’ drama”).
        
*   **DON’T**
    
    *   Use empty hype, big promises, or internet platitudes.
        
    *   Add fluff (“Let’s be real,” “game-changer,” “10x”).
        
    *   Get verbose. If it’s longer than 15 words, cut it or break the line.
        
    *   Sound apologetic or soft-pedal the ask.
        

* * *

### 4. **Bullet & Section Templates**

*   **Bullet Format:**  
    **[Bold 3-5 word benefit/issue]**  
    Short, active explanation. Show the “before/after” or pain/relief.
    
    **Examples:**
    
    *   **Feasibility, Not Fairy Tales**  
        No guesswork—just brutal clarity on what’s actually holding your growth hostage and how to fix it.

* * *

### 5. **ND-Friendly Moves**

*   Make the reader feel _seen_—call out “decision fatigue,” “ops drama,” “real-world overwhelm.”
    
*   Promise _actual_ action/clarity, not “potential.” (“You’ll know what to fix, not just what’s broken.”)
    
*   Remove shame: It’s not their fault, it’s the system/process/tool.
    
*   Always show how they _can_ move forward—even if they “don’t wanna.”
    

* * *

### 6. **CTA Energy**

*   Must be challenge, not plea.
    
*   **Examples:**
    
    *   $1,000 — All fact. No fiction. Forward progress.
        
    *   $500 — Clarity, not corporate. Progress you can feel.
        
    *   $1,000 — Just the fix. No fairy tales.
        
*   Never: “Buy now,” “Sign up today,” or “Limited time!”

## Q: 
What are some effective techniques for prioritizing tasks and managing a busy workload? Remember I’m AuDHD and I’m working in a mixed team with lists of always changing people and chaos to clean up from the lack of communication and clear processes / cross team communications before

## A: 
Given your AuDHD brain, the chaotic nature of your role, and the constant shifting of team members, you need a **flexible but structured** system that accommodates how your brain works while also managing the external chaos. Here are some **effective prioritization techniques** tailored to your situation:

* * *

### **1. The “NOW, NEXT, LATER” Method (Dynamic Prioritization)**

This keeps things fluid and avoids overwhelming decision paralysis.

*   **NOW** → Critical tasks that unblock others or have immediate consequences.
    
*   **NEXT** → Important but not urgent, or tasks that need prep before execution.
    
*   **LATER** → Low-priority or backlogged items that can wait.
    

**Bonus Tip:** Use a **"Waiting On"** section for things you’re blocked on (so you don’t waste mental energy remembering them).

* * *

### **2. The "Impact vs. Effort" Matrix (Kill the Unnecessary Work)**

You’re constantly cleaning up **cross-team miscommunication**, so not every task is worth your energy. Evaluate each task with:

*   **High Impact, Low Effort** → Do ASAP. Quick wins.
    
*   **High Impact, High Effort** → Break into phases/milestones.
    
*   **Low Impact, Low Effort** → Delegate or batch.
    
*   **Low Impact, High Effort** → Question why you’re even doing this.
    

**Real-World Example:** If a task prevents another team from moving forward, it's **High Impact**, even if it’s not your favorite thing to do.

* * *

### **3. Time-Blocking with a Dopamine-Friendly Twist**

Since task-switching is draining, batch similar tasks and give yourself clear "sprints."

*   **💥 Firefighting Mode (90 min max)** → Clear urgent blockers, answer essential messages.
    
*   **🔨 Deep Work Blocks (2-3 hrs max)** → Focused work on high-impact projects.
    
*   **🚀 Quick Wins (30-45 min)** → Knock out small, high-impact tasks.
    
*   **🌊 Buffer Time** → Daily flex time to handle the unexpected.
    

**Dopamine Hack:** Have a "Break the Rules" slot where you work on something fun/unexpected. Helps reset your focus.

* * *

### **4. Pre-Decide Your Response to Chaos**

Instead of constantly reacting, use a **Decision Tree for Chaos**:

*   **Is this MY problem?** If not, delegate.
    
*   **Will this break something if ignored?** If yes, put it in “NOW.”
    
*   **Is this a symptom of a process gap?** If yes, document it for later process improvement.
    

**Why?** It prevents knee-jerk responses that pull you into unnecessary work.

* * *

### **5. The "50% Rule" for Capacity Management**

If you plan **100% of your time**, the unexpected will always derail you.  
👉 **Plan for 50% of your day to be structured** and leave the rest for unexpected fires.

*   **Example:** If you have an 8-hour day, plan 4 hours of structured work and expect 4 hours of interruptions, unexpected requests, and chaos.
    

* * *

### **6. Quick Prioritization for a Swamped Day (5-Minute Reset)**

When the workload is **piling up**, take 5 minutes to do this:

1.  Write down everything causing stress (brain dump).
    
2.  Highlight **3 things that will make today feel successful**.
    
3.  Ignore, reschedule, or delegate the rest.
    

* * *

### **7. Document Fixes for Recurring Chaos (Minimal Viable SOPs)**

You’re cleaning up **cross-team communication messes** → Don’t keep fixing the same problem manually.

*   Keep a **“Process Potholes” list** (things that repeatedly cause chaos).
    
*   Every time you solve something, **document it in 2-3 bullet points** for future reference.
    
*   Eventually, this becomes a **quick-reference doc** for you (and new team members).
    

* * *

### **8. Use “Triage Tags” for Incoming Requests**

When people constantly **dump work on you**, apply **triage tags** instead of immediately reacting.

*   🛑 **"Drop Everything" (Critical, team is blocked)**
    
*   ⏳ **"Time-Sensitive, but Not Urgent" (Schedule a time)**
    
*   🗂 **"Nice to Have, Not Essential" (Backlog it)**
    
*   🚫 **"Why is this even here?" (Challenge or delegate)**
    

* * *

### **9. Have a “Tomorrow Me” List to Reduce Decision Fatigue**

At the end of the day, **write down the 3 tasks “Tomorrow Me” needs to focus on first**.

*   This helps **your brain transition faster** in the morning.
    
*   You **skip decision fatigue** and get a dopamine hit by tackling an already-prepped list.
    

* * *

### **10. Ruthless Task Automation & Delegation**

If you’re **repeating a process manually more than twice**, automate or template it.

**Example:**

*   Use **Zapier** to auto-update shared docs.
    
*   Create **Notion or Google Docs templates** for frequently answered questions.
    
*   **Pre-write response templates** for recurring issues.
    

* * *

### **Final Note:**

You’re juggling **chaotic external factors** and an AuDHD brain that needs **dopamine, structure, and flexibility** at the same time. The key is:

✔ **Pre-decision, not constant decision-making**  
✔ **Having a prioritization "default setting"** so you don’t waste energy choosing  
✔ **Allowing fluidity in your system** so it adapts to shifting chaos

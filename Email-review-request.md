### 2️⃣ Where it might backfire a bit

**a) You’re not naming your hurt clearly enough**

Right now, you’re talking around it:

> “These last few weeks of no response are a boundary you’ve made clear”

That can read as a little passive-aggressive _and_ doesn’t actually say: “This hurt me.”  
Better: say directly that the silence (and hanging mid-project, if you want that in there) has been painful and confusing.

**b) You’re over-owning the blame**

Lines like:

> “That’s especially true if how I show up for you isn’t supportive.”  
> “Or leaves you feeling worse off than better.”  
> “Dread is never a feeling I want to spark…”

Those basically say, “You pulling away must be because I suck,” instead of “Something is off between us and I’d like to understand, without assuming it’s all my fault.”

We want:

*   Open door for her to share if you _did_ hurt her  
    **without**
    
*   Pre-deciding that you are the villain.
    

**c) The PPS has good clarity, but could use one more boundary**

You’re asking:

> “Do you want me to cancel… yes or no? … I’d rather know than hold onto false hope.”

That’s valid. ND-brains do not thrive on ambiguous, forever-open loops.  
I’d add one clean boundary for **what you will do** if she doesn’t respond (e.g., “If I don’t hear back by X, I’ll cancel the invite so I’m not stuck in limbo.”)

That protects _you_ without punishing her.

## Q:

Here’s the real deal (clean and useful, no fluff):

### 🫀 Heart transplant blood type rules

If your brother is **B+**, he can receive a heart from:

*   **B+** ✅ (same type)
    
*   **B−** ✅ (same group, Rh doesn’t block)
    
*   **O+** ✅ (universal donor group for organs)
    
*   **O−** ✅ (most flexible donor)
    

### ❌ Not compatible:

*   **A or AB types** (his immune system would likely reject it)
    

* * *

### Why this works

Blood type matching for organs is about **avoiding immune rejection**, not exact matching.

*   **Type B recipients** can accept from **B and O**
    
*   **Rh factor (+/−)** matters less in organ transplants than in blood transfusions
    

* * *

### Reality check (important)

Blood type is just **one filter**.

They also match:

*   Body size
    
*   Urgency/status on the list
    
*   Distance (time = survival of the organ)
    
*   Tissue compatibility
    

* * *

### Bottom line

Your brother being **B+ actually gives him more options than you think** — not just B+ donors.

*   more fatigue again

> **Past infection that his body successfully fought off — now immune**
